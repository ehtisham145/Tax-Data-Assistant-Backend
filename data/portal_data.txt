Q1:How do I create an invoice?

Go to Invoices → New Invoice. 
Fill in the 6 steps: Invoice Details, Supplier, Buyer, Items, Payment, 
then Submit. Your invoice is assigned a unique number and submitted 
to the ASP automatically.

Q2:What invoice types are supported?

The platform supports:
• Tax Invoice (380)
• Credit Note (381)
• Commercial Invoice (480)
• Continuous Supply Invoice (875)

All per the UAE PINT / PEPPOL BIS 3.0 standard.

Q3: What do the invoice statuses mean?

Draft → editable, not yet submitted.
Pending → submitted, XML being generated.
Submitted → sent to ASP.
Validated → accepted by FTA.
Rejected → FTA found errors; check rejection reason.
Cancelled → voided.
Paid → payment received.

Q4: How do I download the UBL XML?

Open the invoice detail page. Once 
status is Submitted or Validated, a "Download XML" button appears.
 Click it to download the PEPPOL UBL 2.1 XML file.

Q5:How do I issue a credit note?

Create a new invoice and select type "Credit Note (381)". 
Enter the original invoice number in the Reference Number field. Credit notes reverse a 
previously issued tax invoice for returns, cancellations, or corrections.

Q6:What VAT rates are available?

Standard: 5% — applies to most goods & services.
Zero-rated: 0% — exports, international transport, certain food items.
Exempt: no VAT — financial services, residential property.

Q7:What is a TRN?

TRN (Tax Registration Number) is a 15-digit number issued by the UAE 
Federal Tax Authority. It is required
 on every B2B tax invoice for both the seller and buyer.

Q8:What is PEPPOL / UAE PINT?

PEPPOL (Pan-European Public Procurement On-Line) is a global e-invoicing network.
UAE PINT is the UAE national profile based on PEPPOL BIS 3.0
and UBL 2.1, mandated by the UAE FTA for e-invoicing compliance.


Q9: How do I add a company?

Go to Settings → Companies → Add Company. Enter the company name, TRN, address, and contact details. 
You must have at least one active company to create invoices.

Q10: How do I add a customer?

Go to Customers → Add Customer. Fill in the buyer name, TRN (required for B2B),
 address, and contact. Once saved, 
the customer appears in the Buyer picker when creating invoices.

Q11: How do I set up two-factor authentication?

Go to Account → Security → Enable MFA. Scan the QR code 
with an authenticator app (e.g. Google Authenticator).
 Enter the 6-digit code to confirm. MFA adds a secure second login step.

Q12:What user roles exist?

Admin: full access — manage company, users, and all invoices.
Accountant: create and manage invoices.
Viewer: read-only access to invoices.
Inbound Supplier: accesses the supplier portal only.

Q13: Is this platform FTA certified?

Yes. E-Numerak is FTA-certified and fully compliant with 
Federal Decree-Law No. 16 of 2024. It generates PEPPOL BIS 3.0 / 
UBL 2.1 e-invoices and submits them to the Accredited Service Provider (ASP).

Sign up at the E-Invoice Portal, register your company TRN, add your
 customers, and create your

1. General & System Overview
Question: What is the primary objective of the UAE's new e-invoicing system?

Answer: The UAE Ministry of Finance launched the e-invoicing system to make financial transactions more transparent, reliable, and fully compliant with VAT regulations. It aims to standardize business processes and reduce reporting errors or tax fraud.

Question: How does the e-invoicing system operate in the UAE, and what is the role of ASPs?

Answer: The UAE uses a Decentralized Continuous Transaction Controls and Exchange (DCTCE) model. Accredited Service Providers (ASPs) act as trusted intermediaries that validate, send, and receive invoices in near real-time between buyers, sellers, and the Federal Tax Authority.

Question: What is the PINT AE specification and which network does it utilize?

Answer: The PINT AE specification is a globally recognized framework based on the Peppol network. It defines the specific technical format and rules for creating, exchanging, and validating digital invoices within the UAE and internationally.

2. Format & Technical Structure
Question: What is the mandatory file format for a UAE Standard e-Invoice? Can businesses use PDFs?

Answer: All e-invoices must be generated in XML format to ensure they are machine-readable and capable of being processed by automated systems. While businesses may retain a PDF version for internal records, only the XML file is legally valid for official submission.

Question: What is the rule regarding the SpecificationID (IBT-024) in the invoice schema?

Answer: The SpecificationID proves that the invoice adheres strictly to PINT AE rules. It must always begin with the exact string urn:peppol:pint:billing-1@ae-1; otherwise, the invoice will fail system validation and be rejected.

Question: Which standard international codes and formats must be used in a UAE e-invoice?

Answer: Invoices must strictly follow these international standards:

Currencies: ISO 4217 standard codes (e.g., AED, USD).

Countries: ISO 3166-1 Alpha-2 standard codes (e.g., AE, SA).

Units of Measure: UNECE Recommendation 20 (e.g., pieces, litres).

Dates: Formatted strictly as YYYY-MM-DD.

Amounts: Plain numbers with decimals (e.g., 50.00) without text or symbols.

3. Invoice Header & Party Details
Question: What standard fields and identifiers are required in the Invoice Header section?

Answer: The invoice header must contain general identification details, including:

IBT-001: Unique sequential Invoice Number.

IBT-002: Invoice Issue Date.

IBT-003: Invoice Type Code (identifying it as a tax invoice, credit note, or debit note).

IBT-005: Currency Code.

Question: What specific PINT AE codes are used to identify the buyer and seller?

Answer: The buyer and seller identities are validated using the following codes:

IBT-027 / IBT-044: Seller Name and Buyer Name.

IBT-031 / IBT-048: Tax Registration Numbers (TRNs) for VAT compliance.

IBT-034 / IBT-049: Electronic Addresses (endpoints for delivery).

IBG-05 / IBG-08: Postal Address Groups (including specific Emirates codes like DXB or AUH).

4. Mandatory, Optional & Conditional Fields
Question: What are "Mandatory Fields" in a UAE e-invoice, and what happens if one is missing?

Answer: Mandatory fields are non-negotiable details required for an invoice to legally exist. If any mandatory field—such as the Invoice Number (IBT-001), Issue Date (IBT-002), TRNs (IBT-031/048), Taxable Amount (IBT-116), VAT Amount (IBT-117), or Grand Total (IBT-112)—is missing, the invoice fails validation and cannot be exchanged.

Question: What purpose do "Optional Fields" serve in the PINT AE framework?

Answer: Optional fields are not strictly required for tax compliance but provide additional business context and transparency for records and auditing. Examples include the Actual Delivery Date (IBT-072), Item Attributes (IBG-32), and Country of Origin for goods (IBT-159).

Question: What are "Conditional Fields"? Provide examples of when they become compulsory.

Answer: Conditional fields are default optional fields that become strictly mandatory under specific business scenarios. For example:

Exchange Rate (BTUAE-04): Required when the invoice currency is not AED.

Service Accounting Code (BTUAE-17): Required when invoicing specifically for services.

Tax Total in Accounting Currency (IBT-111): Required during multi-currency accounting transactions.

5. Financial Breakdown & Calculations
Question: How are products and services distinctly classified under the line items section?

Answer: Each product or service must be listed on a separate line. Physical goods are classified using an HSN Code (IBT-158), while services are classified using a SAC Code (BTUAE-17).

Question: What data points must be included under the Tax Breakdown section of the invoice?

Answer: The tax breakdown must clearly detail how VAT is applied using:

IBT-116: Taxable Amount (value before tax).

IBT-117: Tax Amount (actual VAT charged).

IBT-118: Tax Category Code (Standard, Zero-rated, Exempt, or Reverse Charge).

IBT-119: Tax Rate (the applicable VAT percentage).

Question: What codes represent the total calculations and final payment methods on an e-invoice?

Answer: Financial summaries are tracked via IBT-109 (Total without VAT), IBT-110 (Total VAT amount), IBT-112 (Grand total including VAT), and IBT-115 (Final payable amount). The payment method is designated via the Payment Means Code (IBT-081), covering options like bank transfers or cards.

6. Validation & Cardinality Rules
Question: What is the "Cardinality Rule" in the PINT AE schema, and how is it structured?

Answer: The cardinality rule governs field logic, explicitly defining how often any data field can appear within an XML invoice:

1..1: The field must appear exactly once.

0..1: The field is optional, but if included, it can only appear once.

0..n: The field is optional and can be repeated multiple times (such as adding multiple line items or tax tiers).
